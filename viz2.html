Do the attributes of pokemons decide their usage rate?

In the battle, we definitely want to have Each pokemon's 6 basic attributes: HP, Attack, Defense, Special Attack, Special Defense and Speed as large as possible. In this part, we are more focused on the statistical distribution of attributes of pokemons to see whether the pokemons' usage rate in pokemon battles is positively related to their attributes.

Tiers

We believe that a pokemon that is used more frequently is more useful. This usage rate is what the Tier stands for. The Tier of pokemons consists of values: 'Uber', 'RU', 'OU', 'UUBL','UU','RUBL', 'RU', 'NUBL', 'NU','PUBL', 'PU' and 'LC'. Since 'Uber' represents the pokemons that are banned using and 'LC' represents the pokemons that are too weak to be taken into account when players are arranging their teams, we only use the left tiers. Therefore, the left ones are 'OU', 'UUBL','UU','RUBL', 'RU','NUBL', 'NU','PUBL' and'PU', where 'OU' represents the most frequently used ones and 'PU' the least. And we generally only focus on pokemons in Tier "OU". Then, it is common to believe the total of 6 basic attributes reveals a pokemon's power. The higher of total number is, the stronger pokemon is revealed. Therefore, we want to visualize the relation between Tier and the attributes as follows. We can see it almost meets our expectations. For all 6 attributes of pokemons, we can see that all pokemons in OU have higher amount of 6 attributes.

Does the later generation have pokemons with higher attributes?

Generation Distribution

Generally, a game series will introduce more powerful figures(or characters) when they promote a new generation in order that gamers want to follow the new generation. The later a pokemon is introduced, its possibility of being stronger is higher as well. In order to see whether pokemon games also obey this routine, we will illustrate the changes of each generations. You can use buttion to see the changes in the mean value of each attribute of pokemons as well as the base total of them. (Base total = HP + Attack + Defense + Special Attack + Special Defense + Speed)



We can see that despite the 4th generation has a specially high number in mean figures, the trends of pokemon games obey the mentioned routine.
